The Thread is Broken

Marie Laveau is dead. For eight years, the boundary between the living and the dead has been eroding. Spirits walk the streets of New Orleans, confused and hostile. The old wards are failing. Someone needs to hold the thread.

You are a Whisperman. A voodoo gunslinger, bound to the lwa, armed with a revolver and the sacred veve. You stand at the crossroads between worlds.

Two Games. One Universe.

WMTX

Tactical

Turn-based squad combat. Position your Whispermen, draw veve for spiritual attacks, manage your lwa bonds. Every decision has weight. Every spirit remembers.

WMFPS

First Person

Revolver in one hand, veve traced with the other. A gesture-based casting system built from real Vodou ritual. No spell menus. You perform the magic.

Rooted in Real Vodou

Not voodoo dolls. Not zombie powder. We went to ethnographic sources and built our game systems from the actual tradition.

The Lwa

Rada, Petwo, Gede. Three nanchon. Not good vs evil. Temperature. Your patron lwa shapes your abilities, your personality, your fate. Go deep enough and the spirit rides you.

Veve Casting

Sacred geometric symbols drawn to invoke the lwa. In-game, you trace them by hand. Each pattern channels a different spirit's power. Ritual as mechanic.

Gris-Gris

Charm bags with herbs, oils, stones, and prayers. A crafting system with cultural roots. Every component carries traditional significance.

Baron Samedi

Lord of the dead. Your narrator. Drunk, irreverent, hilarious, and terrifying. He decides who lives and who dies. Think Darkest Dungeon narrator, but with rum.

The Five Attributes

No generic stats. Every attribute maps to the Vodou tradition.

Fury Petwo

Aggression, force, revolution. The hot spirits fuel your wrath.

Grace Rada

Healing, protection, ancestry. The cool spirits guide your hand.

Sight Gede

Death, truth, the crossroads. The liminal spirits open your eyes.

Resolve Cross-Nanchon

Willpower. The strength to hold the thread when spirits pull.

Communion Spiritual Depth

Your bond with the lwa. Deeper communion means greater power, and greater cost.
