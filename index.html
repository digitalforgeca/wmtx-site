The Pitch

XCOM meets voodoo frontier

Command a squad of supernatural investigators through turn-based tactical missions on the Louisiana frontier. Draw sacred veves for battlefield power. Manage herbs, silver, and spirit. Listen to Baron Samedi tell you how badly you're doing.

The year is 1889. Eight years since Marie Laveau's death. The boundary between the living and the dead is eroding — and something is pushing through from the other side.

You lead a ragtag squad of mortals touched by the lwa — voodoo spirits who grant power in exchange for service. Every mission takes you deeper into the bayou, closer to the crossroads, and further from anything resembling safety.

This is not a game about conquering the supernatural. It's about surviving it.

Combat System

Every angle matters. Every bullet counts.

⬡ Gridless Tactical Combat

No grid. No tiles. Continuous 3D space on Terrain3D geometry. Ruler-based movement with NavMesh pathfinding. Movement radius rings show your reachable area in real time. Terrain type multipliers change the cost of every step — bayou water costs 2× movement, roads only 0.75×.

◎ Raycast Cover System

Forget binary half/full cover. WMTX calculates cover as a percentage by casting 16 rays from attacker to target body volume. A fence with gaps, a tree trunk, a crumbled wall — each provides different coverage depending on your exact angle of approach. Move two feet to the left and the math changes entirely.

💥 Destructible Cover

Geometry takes damage and breaks. That stone wall protecting your herbalist? A few shotgun blasts will reduce it to rubble — and your cover percentages update dynamically as the battlefield transforms around you.

⛰ Continuous Elevation

No discrete levels. The heightmap is continuous — a gentle slope gives you a slight advantage, a church bell tower gives you a commanding one. Height advantage improves hit chance naturally through raycast geometry. Physics, not abstractions.

⚡ Action Points

Each unit has AP per turn. Move, attack, draw a veve, set overwatch, reload, use items — everything costs AP. Spend wisely. A unit that blows all AP on movement is defenseless. A unit that conserves AP can overwatch and punish enemy movement.

👁 Overwatch & Reaction Fire

Reserve AP and set a watch arc. When enemies move through your cone of fire, automatic reaction shots trigger. Stack multiple overwatches to create kill corridors. But beware — enemies can overwatch too.

⚔ Engagement Zones

Melee enemies create threat zones around themselves. Walking out of an engagement zone costs AP — and provokes a free attack. Positioning isn't just about cover; it's about controlling who can move where.

🎬 Action Camera

Cinematic over-the-shoulder camera sequences for attacks and critical moments. XCOM-style dramatic shots that make every kill feel weighty and every miss feel devastating. The bayou has never looked this dangerous.

"Ah, you moved your gunslinger into the open. Bold. Stupid, but bold. Let's see if the zombies appreciate your bravery."

— Baron Samedi

The Veve System

Sacred geometry. Drawn in blood and bayou dust.

Veves are sacred geometric symbols drawn to invoke specific lwa mid-combat. Each veve is an investment of Action Points, Spirit, and herbs — and once drawn, they persist on the battlefield for multiple turns as powerful area effects.

But power has a price. Taking damage while drawing can cause the veve to fail — or worse, backfire. Enemy practitioners can draw counter-veves. And overlapping veve radii create compound effects that change the rules of engagement entirely.

The Six Veves

🔥

Ogoun's Fire

Fire AoE Damage

Scorches a battlefield zone with divine fire. Enemies in the radius take burn damage each turn. Pairs devastatingly with Marinette's Thorns.

💧

Erzulie's Grace

Healing Zone

A sanctuary of restorative energy. Allies within the radius heal over time. The only reliable way to recover mid-mission without consumables.

🚪

Legba's Gateway

Movement Boost

Opens pathways between spaces. Allies in the zone gain bonus movement AP and can traverse difficult terrain at normal cost.

👤

Baron's Shadow

Accuracy Debuff

Shrouds the area in death's shadow. Enemies within suffer severe accuracy penalties. Stack with cover for near-untouchable positions.

🐍

Damballah's Ward

Armor Zone

The Great Serpent's protection manifests as a shimmering ward. Allies in the zone gain temporary armor that absorbs incoming damage.

🌿

Marinette's Thorns

Heavy Fire Damage + Friendly Fire

Unleashes wild, uncontrolled fire damage in a wide radius. Devastating — but doesn't discriminate. Your units caught inside will burn. Use with extreme caution.

Veve Mechanics

Persistence

Drawn veves stay on the battlefield for multiple turns, creating lasting area effects that shape the flow of combat.

Combo Effects

Overlapping veve radii create compound effects. Fire + Water = steam and obscurement. Ward + Gateway = fortified advance lanes.

Counter-Veves

Enemy practitioners can draw their own veves — turning your sanctuary into a contested zone or neutralizing your effects entirely.

Disruption Risk

Taking damage while drawing a veve can cause it to fail or backfire. Protect your caster — or pay the price.

Required Herbs

Basil Rue Sage Graveyard Dust Bayou Moss Crossroads Dirt Blood Orchid

The Six Lwa

Patron spirits of the crossroads. Choose wisely — they choose back.

Each squad member bonds with a patron lwa — a voodoo spirit who grants supernatural abilities in exchange for devotion. Your lwa shapes your playstyle, your talent tree, and the very nature of your power. The spirits are not tools. They are partners. And they have opinions.

Baron Samedi

Loa of Death, Passage & The Grave

☠ Death ■ Purple

The Lord of the Dead doesn't just flirt with mortality — he weaponizes it. Baron-bonded units thrive on the edge of death, harvesting spirit from the fallen and bargaining with forces that would destroy lesser minds. Dark bargains, death triggers, and an unnerving survivability.

Playstyle: Spirit Economy & Death Triggers

Death's Doorstep

Survive a fatal blow at 1 HP. Once per mission. Death has to wait.

Gravedigger's Toll

Mark an enemy — when they die, harvest bonus spirit from their passage.

Gallows Humor

Gain dodge bonus when at low HP. The closer to death, the harder to hit.

Ogoun

Loa of War, Iron & Fire

🔥 Fire ■ Crimson

The Iron Warrior. Ogoun-bonded units are frontline combatants who get tougher as the battle wears on. They armor up when wounded, inspire allies with war cries, and turn the forge of combat into raw power. When the bullets fly, Ogoun's children walk forward.

Playstyle: Frontline Combat & Armor

Iron Skin

Gain temporary armor when wounded. Pain makes you stronger.

War Cry

Buff all allies' aim within hearing range. Iron sharpens iron.

Iron Will

Build spirit reserves through sustained combat. The longer the fight, the deeper the well.

Erzulie

Loa of Love, Grace & Water

💧 Water ■ Azure

Grace under fire. Erzulie-bonded units are the heart of any squad — healers, protectors, and surprisingly dangerous when their allies are threatened. They move through danger with preternatural elegance and mend what the bayou breaks.

Playstyle: Healing & Protective Retaliation

Graceful Step

Evade overwatch fire on first move each turn. Grace is its own armor.

Soothing Touch

Heal an adjacent ally for a moderate amount. Costs AP, not consumables.

Water Affinity

Draw water-element veves at reduced spirit cost. The river flows through you.

Legba

Loa of Crossroads, Pathways & Spirit

✦ Spirit ■ Gold

The Gatekeeper. Legba-bonded units control the flow of battle itself — opening paths that shouldn't exist, seeing enemies before they're visible, and bending the action economy to breaking point. If Ogoun wins fights, Legba decides which fights happen.

Playstyle: Mobility & Action Economy

Pathfinder

Gain extra AP on the first turn of combat. First mover advantage — the crossroads favor the bold.

Crossroads Sense

Extended vision range. See further, react sooner, ambush effectively.

Spirit Walk

Teleport to a visible position within range. The crossroads are everywhere.

Damballah

Loa of Wisdom, Protection & The Serpent

🐍 Serpent ■ Silver

The Ancient Serpent. Damballah-bonded units are patient, precise, and devastatingly effective when properly positioned. They reward staying still, thinking ahead, and turning defense into an art form. Where others rush, the serpent waits.

Playstyle: Overwatch & Patient Precision

Serpent's Patience

Gain accuracy bonus when you haven't moved this turn. Stillness is power.

Coil Ward

Shield an adjacent ally, redirecting a portion of incoming damage to yourself.

Ancient Wisdom

Chance to double the effect of drawn veves. The old knowledge flows deep.

Marinette

Loa of Ferocity, Freedom & Fire

🔥 Fire ■ Blaze

The Wild One. Marinette-bonded units are barely controlled chaos — highest damage, highest risk, highest reward. They move first, hit hardest, and set everything on fire. Including, occasionally, your own squad. She doesn't care. Freedom has a cost.

Playstyle: Aggressive Fire & Scorched Earth

Feral Instinct

High initiative — always acts early in the turn order. The wild strike first.

Savage Charge

Rush toward an enemy and gain a free melee attack. Close the gap, deliver the violence.

Wild Fire

Boosted fire veve effects. When Marinette's children draw fire, it burns hotter and wider.

Advancement System

Two axes. Sixty combinations. No two squads alike.

Every squad member advances along two axes: their Archetype (mortal profession) and their Lwa (spiritual patron). 5 archetypes × 6 lwa × 2 temperaments = 60 unique build combinations. And that's before you start making talent choices.

☽ Rada Path

Cool. Measured. Defensive.

The Rada temperament emphasizes patience, protection, and precision. Rada abilities tend toward defensive buffs, sustained healing, accuracy bonuses, and controlled area effects. Same lwa — completely different playstyle. The Rada path asks: how long can you endure?

☀ Petwo Path

Hot. Fierce. Aggressive.

The Petwo temperament is fire and fury — aggressive action economy, burst damage, risk-reward abilities, and scorched-earth tactics. Same lwa, but Petwo burns brighter and faster. The Petwo path asks: can you end it before it ends you?

Tiered Talent Trees

At each tier, pick 2 of 3 available talents. You can't have everything — every build has deliberate gaps. This creates meaningful choices where specialization matters and no single unit can do it all. The system is fully declarative and data-driven: all effects defined in JSON, fully moddable.

The Five Archetypes

🎯

Gunslinger

Ranged Specialist

Accuracy and crit growth. The sharpshooter. Gunslingers excel at precision fire from optimal range, scoring critical hits that can turn a battle. Their damage scales with patience and positioning.

🌿

Herbalist

Spirit-Focused Support

High max spirit and regen. The veve specialist. Herbalists keep the squad alive and empowered, drawing veves at reduced cost and maintaining spirit reserves that would exhaust any other archetype.

✦

Ritualist

Pure Caster

Highest spirit pool and regen in the game. The master of the veve arts. Ritualists can chain multiple veves per mission, maintain overlapping effects, and push the boundaries of what the lwa will grant.

👊

Brawler

Frontline Tank

HP and armor growth. The melee damage powerhouse. Brawlers close the distance and make enemies regret it. Their engagement zones lock down threats, and they get tougher with every scar.

💨

Scout

Mobile Flanker

Dodge and initiative growth. Extra AP at higher levels. Scouts dart through the battlefield, flanking enemies, spotting threats, and creating opportunities for the rest of the squad to exploit.

"A Petwo Brawler bonded to Marinette. You've created a walking inferno with fists. I approve — from a safe distance."

The Threats

The bayou doesn't send warnings. It sends teeth.

🧟

Zombies

Undead — Melee Swarm

Slow. Tough. Relentless. They come in numbers, absorbing fire and closing distance with mindless determination. One zombie is a nuisance. Eight zombies surrounding your squad is a crisis.

🧟‍♂️

3-Head Zombie

Undead — Mini-Boss

45 HP. Armored. Multi-attack with a massive threat radius. Three heads means three sets of teeth and three chances to ruin your positioning. Prioritize or suffer.

🤠

Hostile Cowboys

Human — Ranged Combatants

They use cover intelligently. They flank. They overwatch. These aren't shambling undead — they're trained killers with six-shooters and bad intentions. Respect them or die.

💀

Brutes

Heavy — Armored Area Denial

Massive HP pools. Heavy armor. Devastating melee damage. Brutes don't chase — they hold ground, creating zones of death that force you to find another way around.

🔮

Hostile Practitioners

Caster — Enemy Veve Users

High initiative. They draw counter-veves that neutralize your effects or create hostile zones. The highest priority target on any battlefield — kill them first, or fight on their terms.

🦇

Bats & Insects

Swarm — Cloud Area Denial

Swarm entities that create clouds of biting, clawing chaos. They don't hit hard individually, but they obscure vision, reduce accuracy, and make you miserable.

🧛

Vampires (Garkain)

Undead — Fast Flankers

Terrifyingly fast. They close distance before you can react, bypassing your front line to strike at vulnerable units. Vulnerable to silver — if you remembered to bring it.

"The Garkain are hungry tonight. I'd recommend silver bullets — but watching you figure that out the hard way is more entertaining."

— Baron Samedi

Devastating at close range. The spread pattern means you don't need perfect aim — just proximity and nerve. Two barrels. Two chances to make an impression.

The Crossroads

Your settlement. Your sanctuary. Your staging ground.

The Crossroads isn't a menu — it's a living, growing 3D settlement. Buildings appear as you unlock them. NPCs wander the streets. The town evolves with your progress, growing from a handful of shacks into a fortified frontier outpost.

📋 Missions Board

Choose your next deployment from available missions. Story missions advance the campaign; side missions provide resources, intel, and squad experience. Every choice shapes what comes next.

🏪 Vendor

Buy weapons, ammo, herbs, gear, and consumables. Stock refreshes between missions. Silver currency earned from missions and salvage. Invest wisely — the bayou doesn't do refunds.

👥 Squad Management

Heal wounded squad members. Equip gear and weapons. Level up and choose talents. Assign lwa bonds. Review stats and build paths. Your squad is only as strong as your preparation.

💀 Baron's Corner

Baron Samedi holds court. Receive cryptic advice, commentary on recent missions, and occasionally useful information buried under layers of sarcasm. He's helpful. In his way.

⚗ Crafting

Combine herbs into veve components, craft blessed ammunition, create warding charms. Recipes unlock as you progress. The difference between a prepared squad and a dead one is what you craft before the mission.

🏗 Facility Upgrades

Build and upgrade facilities to unlock new crafting recipes, vendor stock, training options, and settlement capabilities. The Crossroads grows with you — invest in its future.

Baron Samedi — The Host

The Loa of Death is watching. And he has notes.

Baron Samedi isn't just a lwa patron — he's the AI Director, recontextualized from the Whispermen FPS as your sardonic, theatrical, omniscient tactical commentator.

He watches every battle. He reacts to every decision. First blood, flanking kills, multi-kills, clutch moments, squad wipes — Baron has something to say about all of it. His mood shifts with the battle's momentum, and his commentary is context-aware with template variables that reference specific units, enemies, and situations.

A priority system ensures critical story moments always take precedence over ambient commentary, but during the quiet stretches between the chaos, Baron fills the silence with observations that are equal parts amusing, unsettling, and occasionally helpful.

"First blood! And it's yours! Oh, this is going to be a wonderful evening."

"A flanking kill with the sawed-off. Messy. Effective. I'd applaud, but I'm saving my hands for your eulogy."

"Three dead in one turn. You're either very skilled or very lucky. Let's find out which."

"Your herbalist just drew a veve while standing in fire. That's not bravery — that's a cry for help."

"Squad wipe. Beautiful. I'll send flowers — to myself, for having to watch that."

Multiplayer

The dead don't discriminate. Neither does the competition.

🌐 Steam Networking Sockets + LAN

Full multiplayer via Valve's Steam Networking Sockets for online play, with ENet for LAN connections. Low latency, reliable, and built on industry-standard networking.

📜 Command Protocol

10 validated command types ensure deterministic combat across all clients. Every action is validated, synchronized, and reproducible. No desync. No cheating. No arguments about what happened.

🎲 Seeded RNG

Random outcomes use seeded RNG for fair, reproducible results. Both players see the same rolls. The universe is consistent — even when it's cruel.

🏛 Lobby System

Squad validation and match configuration before combat begins. Verify builds, set rules, configure the arena. No surprises — except the tactical kind.

Same codebase handles singleplayer and multiplayer — only the authority layer changes. Play the campaign solo. Challenge a friend. The bayou doesn't care how many people it swallows.

Content Packs & Modding

The lwa welcome new voices.

WMTX is built to be modded from day one. Drop-in content packs — put a folder in content/, the game picks it up. The same format powers first-party DLC and community mods. No special tools needed. No compilation. Just data and creativity.
